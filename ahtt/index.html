Ash's Hot Tech Takes

Introduction

Welcome to my website. I have some thoughts about technology that I'd like to share on the Internet, mainly just as a way of getting it written down. I'd post on Twitter but I don't think it's very intuitive for long-form discussion.

On this website, most of my 'tech takes' will actually be gaming takes. It's just that I'll occassionally dip my feet into tech-related subjects so it felt like it would fit better if I labelled the website as 'tech takes'.

I think with some of these articles, I'll definitely try to go for a comedy approach with them depending on when I write them. The more annoyed I am about something, the less jokes will appear. It also depends on my effort levels at the time.

Me

I'm Ash - a first year university student and I'll be the first to admit that there's not a lot of research going into these articles, but maybe that's a good thing. Maybe the impressions of someone who doesn't really know the true reasons behind certain choices is enough to make a comment on the suitability of those choices in the first place. I like games, and I like programming. I also like programming games.

Because of this, I have awarded myself the privilege of spouting my ill-informed opinions on several technology matters onto the internet in the form of a shoddily-prepared website. Please enjoy yourselves.

Website Q&A

  • Why does this look like it was made in the 90s?
    Because I can't be bothered to make nice CSS for this website. It's here for text content and nothing more.

  • Why is this website not using Wix/Wordpress/Whatever website builder is popular these days?
    I considered using Wordpress but found myself not particularly liking any .wordpress.com URLs. I could have, and it would have been easier, but I liked the idea of having it on my Github page anyway.

  • Do I care?
    No. I just like the idea of someone randomly stumbling across this on the Internet.

  • Can I make the website look nicer?
    I'll happily do the bare minimum to make the website functional but it's a push to be expecting anything particularly fancy. I imagine it'll look closer to a 90s webpage if I do stylise it. I like that style.

  • How am I developing this website?
    Notepad.
